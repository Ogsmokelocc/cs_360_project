Insider Trader Detection

Two unsupervised methods compared — neither knows who the actual insiders are.

Research
DATASET — users — finished events — settled bets — true insiders Seed Data
Methods
Z-ScoreStatistical Baseline
Extracts 4 features per user, normalises each with Z-scores, and sums them into a composite suspicion score. Users are ranked highest-to-lowest. Heuristic — it measures, but doesn't truly learn.
K-MeansUnsupervised ML
Runs k-means (k=2) with k-means++ initialisation on min-max normalised features. Iteratively discovers two natural clusters. The cluster with the higher mean win rate is labelled "suspected insiders" — no labels used during training.
Shared features: Win Rate · Avg ROI · Late Bet Ratio (% of bets placed <4 hr before event start) · Stake Consistency (inverted coefficient of variation)
